1. Get seriously into meditation: Attend multi-day retreats in each of the areas listed on the Hermes Amara website (which could be condensed into four: Samādhi/Jhāna, Emptiness/Ways of Looking, Mettā, and Soulmaking). I can recommend retreats with Gaia House and Nirodha

  2. Take up endurance sports: Enter a swimrun, triathlon or long-distance running, cycling or swimming race

  3. Keep a journal: Buy a quality journal and pen and aim to write a few pages every week

  4. Read widely: Including a healthy dose of protopian fiction like Retrotopia, A Psalm for the Wild-Built, Another Life and classics like Island and The Dispossessed

  5. Sing regularly: Join a choir, take singing lessons or attend an event like Voice Camp

  6. Learn a language: Start by finding the most common 5000 words in your target language and use AI to turn them into Anki flashcards

  7. Start a newsletter: Email is the original open, decentralised protocol. Having the email addresses of followers means you can move to another platform at will, without needing to rebuild your following. Options include Substack and Ghost

  8. Use the best versions of the latest AIs: Current top models are orders of magnitude more capable than those from a couple of years ago, and are also much more capable than current free versions. Use AI to help you write a book, create a film or make an album.

  9. Host an event: Run an event, online or offline, on a topic you’re passionate about. List it on Dandelion (no platform fees, run by a not-for-profit worker co-operative, fair source and open data)

  10. Start a co-op with your friends: Become your own bosses and work on something truly meaningful to you

  11. Join the Green Party (if you’re in the UK): The next UK election is just 3 years away, and for the first time ever, a Green-led government is a realistic possibility–but a Green win will require your participation

  12. Visit prefigurative spaces: Spend some time at places prototyping new ways of living and being like Brave Earth, Emerge Lakefront, Casa Tilo, Feÿtopia and Life Itself

  13. (Yet) Call somewhere home: Being a digital nomad was fun but it doesn’t compare to really planting roots somewhere (for me that somewhere was Totnes, now Stockholm). Prepare for the possibility that international travel becomes more difficult and expensive over the coming years.

  14. Get married: Something deep in my being has relaxed since getting married. describes it as a ‘magic spell’. Plus the wedding was incredibly fun.

  15. Have kids: Yes, I’m now a dad ☺️ Our daughter Runa was born on 2 March. So far I’m resonating with Having Kids Has Been Far Easier and Better than I Expected
